    • EARLY CHILDHOOD
    When I was around my early childhood I was not that much fond of studying. All I wanted to do was watch telivision all to the point where my mother would put up a curfew on my watching hours. It made me mad and I acted...well like a child. I threw a tantrum which I regretted right after learning to listen the hard way. My mother disciplined me which I am thankful for because of her I didn't grew up spoiled.

    A few years after that I learned to entertain myself by drawing. I drew at every book, notebook, or paper that I saw until I fell asleep. I came to like drawing and it became my hobby even until now.
    ELEMENTARY DAYS
    I spent a decent amount of my elementary days being an extrovert. This is where I learned to communicate with others, learn how to make friends and gaining different kinds of achievements outside of my home. I was still fond of drawing and in my elementary days I found people with the same passion. We drew comics together and even made our own characters.

    When I was in my 5th grade I joined my first science quiz bee which is what made me begin to like studying. Ever since then I was always included in the honor rolls which I took pride in although one subject I always had a hard time dealing with was Mathematics.
    JUNIOR HIGH DAYS
    During my Junior Highschool Days I made mistakes A LOT and I regretted them A LOT but that just means I also learned plenty from those mistakes. I experienced a different schedule of going to school and going home so it made me excited.

    In grade 8 we had to go home at night and I'd find the time where me and my friends walking home while laughing to be a precious time. I truly enjoyed my Junior Highschool Days as I slowly prepare for Senior High.
    SENIOR HIGH DAYS
    As a Senior highschool student I became a step closer to entering the real world of adults. I came face-to-face with deadlines, found myself having a hard time managing my time, and worst of all is that math is still difficult! But even if I find it quite tiring it wasn't always hard.

    I found myself relying on others and I began to deepen my bonds with others ultimately cherishing the time I spend with them. I am once again thankful that I met new friends and supportive teachers that sees us as adults and not kids.
    ACHIEVMENTS
    Over the years I've made achievments both small and big. A few of them is gaining impressive grades, winning competitions, graduating from elementary and highschool and being alive to see today's sunset. I couldn't possibly have done it without God's help and the support of those people that I met. Looking forward for the incoming years I wish to achieve more and If it's not too much too ask I'd also like to have people to celebrate those achievments with.
